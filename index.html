Why Financial Literacy Matters

Financial literacy is the blend of awareness, knowledge, skills, attitudes, and behaviours needed to make sound financial decisions and achieve long-term financial well-being. It’s about confidently meeting today’s financial needs while staying resilient for the future.
Sources: CFPB (2015), Muir et al. (2017), FCAC (2019)

📊 The Reality Today

  • Debt: Canadians carry approximately $1.74 in household debt for every dollar of disposable income.
  • Emergency Funds: About 1 in 4 North American adults cannot cover a $400 unexpected expense.
  • Lack of Confidence: Nearly 40% of adults feel anxious or overwhelmed when managing their finances.
